Brewing Tips

🍂 Black Tea Brewing Tips

  • Use fresh boiling water (100°C / 212°F).
  • Add 1 tsp of tea leaves per cup.
  • Steep for 3–5 minutes (longer for stronger flavor).
  • Best enjoyed plain or with milk/sugar.
  • Avoid over-steeping to prevent bitterness.

🍃 Green Tea Brewing Tips

  • Heat water to 75–85°C (not boiling).
  • Use 1 tsp of green tea leaves per cup.
  • Steep for 2–3 minutes only.
  • Use filtered or spring water for best taste.
  • Can be enjoyed plain or with lemon/honey.

🌿 Herbal Tea Brewing Tips

  • Use boiling water (96–100°C).
  • Add 1–2 tsp of herbal blend per cup.
  • Steep for 5–7 minutes (longer for stronger infusion).
  • No caffeine – ideal for nighttime sipping.
  • Optional: Add honey or lemon for flavor.

🍁 Assam Tea Brewing Tips

  • Use boiling water (100°C).
  • Add 1 tsp of Assam tea leaves per cup.
  • Steep for 4–5 minutes.
  • Strong and malty – pairs well with milk and sugar.
  • Perfect for chai or breakfast tea.

🌶️ Masala Chai Brewing Tips

  • Boil 1 cup water + 1 cup milk together.
  • Add 1–1.5 tsp black tea leaves.
  • Add spices (cardamom, ginger, cloves, cinnamon).
  • Boil for 3–5 minutes, simmer on low flame.
  • Strain and sweeten with sugar/jaggery.

❄️ White Tea Brewing Tips

  • Use water at 70–80°C (never boiling).
  • Add 1–2 tsp of white tea leaves per cup.
  • Steep for 4–5 minutes.
  • Delicate flavor – no milk or sugar needed.
  • Best served in glass/ceramic teaware.

🌿 Tulsi Tea Brewing Tips

  • Use freshly boiled water (90–100°C).
  • Add 1–2 tsp of dried tulsi leaves or a tea bag.
  • Steep for 5–6 minutes.
  • Naturally caffeine-free and calming.
  • Drink warm, with honey or lemon if preferred.

🌄 Nilgiri Tea Brewing Tips

  • Use boiling water (100°C).
  • Add 1 tsp of Nilgiri tea leaves per cup.
  • Steep for 3–4 minutes.
  • Known for fruity and floral notes.
  • Tastes great plain or with milk.

☕ Fun Facts About Tea

  • 🍵 Tea is the second most consumed beverage in the world — only water beats it!
  • 😅 The word "chai" means "tea" in Hindi — so “chai tea” is actually “tea tea”!
  • 🌱 White tea is the least processed and comes from the youngest leaves and buds.
  • 😂 Spill tea on your keyboard and you’ve made true “Java” script!
  • 🌸 Nilgiri tea from South India’s Blue Mountains has a floral aroma prized in blends.
  • 🌏 Assam, India is the world’s largest tea-growing region, producing strong malty teas.
  • 🏺 Green tea was first used as medicine in China over 4,000 years ago.
  • 🥛 Adding milk to tea started in Europe, not India — yet milk tea is now a South Asian classic.
